In fall 2023 we were approached for a redesign of an internal application with a few hundred users. For this project, I was in charge of research and user interviews, as well as leading the concept and design phase. 

As is the nature of a redesign, we had to support existing workflows first and foremost, but there were some new features planned as well and together with the customer we agreed to some deviations from the current processes.

## Research 

As almost all of our users are experts in their field (but not necessarily experts of the application, see later), it was especially important to gather qualitative feedback of the current version. To get a comprehensive overview, we conducted a number of interviews, user tests and shadowed users of different teams, so we would be able to get as many perspectives as possible. Our main goals were to find out:

- What are the users' tasks?
- How do they accomplish their tasks?
- How well can they accomplish them?
- If so, what issues do they encounter?

Since some users use this application for a big part of their daily work, they also had some opinions and were able to give feedback and hints, which we included in our findings. 

> The users of this application – a small group of experts – made gathering qualitative feedback especially important.

We mapped out the application sorted the findings according to where they appear on our map[^1]. We also categorized our findings into ideas, problems/opportunities and what works well.
